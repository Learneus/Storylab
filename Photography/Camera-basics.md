Camera Basics for Beginners

Introduction
Understanding camera basics is important for taking better photos. Three key settings control exposure in photography.

1. ISO
ISO controls how sensitive your camera sensor is to light.

Low ISO (100–200)
- Best for bright environments
- Produces less noise

High ISO (800+)
- Useful in low light
- May produce grainy images

2. Shutter Speed
Shutter speed determines how long the camera sensor is exposed to light.

Fast shutter speed
- Freezes motion

Slow shutter speed
- Creates motion blur

3. Aperture
Aperture controls how much light enters the camera lens.

Wide aperture (f/1.8 – f/2.8)
- Blurred background
- Great for portraits

Narrow aperture (f/8 – f/16)
- More of the scene in focus
- Good for landscapes

Conclusion
Mastering ISO, shutter speed, and aperture will greatly improve your photography skills.
